# Teapot model of Chinese web graph has been included in a Web 2.0 book

In a study on hyperlinks among 800+ million webpages in mainland China, Jonathan J. H. Zhu and his collaborators at Peking University proposed a Teapot model to characterize the topological structure of Chinese web graph (Zhu et al., 2008). The model has been selected by Paul Anderson to include in his forthcoming book "'Web 2.0: Principles and Technologies", to be published by CRC/Chapman Hall. Anderson is an award winner of the EPSRC Computer Science Writer of the Year 2007.
