# Two papers accepted by 2011WAPOR conference

Lu, H., Peng, T. Q., & Zhu, J. J. H. (2011). The effect of the Internet on national identity: A cohort study in Hong Kong. The 64th Annual Conference of the World Association for Public Opinion Research (WAPOR), Amsterdam, The Netherlands, September. Wang,C.J.(2011).The Emergence of Spiral of Silence: Agent-based Modeling of the Spiral of Silence. The 64th Annual Conference of the World Association for Public Opinion Research (WAPOR), Amsterdam, The Netherlands, September. The **World Association for Public Opinion Research** (**WAPOR**) is an [international](<http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_nongovernmental_organization> "International nongovernmental organization") [professional association](<http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Professional_association>) of [researchers](<http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Researcher> "Researcher") in the fields of[communication](<http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Communication_studies> "Communication studies") and [survey research](<http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Survey_research>). It is a member organization of the [International Social Science Council](<http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Social_Science_Council>). Established in 1947, the association acquired its current name in 1948, at the Third International Conference on Public Opinion Research. In 1953, it became the sole nongovernment consultant organization to [UNESCO](<http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UNESCO>) in the field of polling. Its current [president](<http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/President#Non-governmental_presidents>) is [Tom W. Smith](<http://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Tom_W._Smith&action=edit&redlink=1> "Tom W. Smith \(page does not exist\)") ([NORC](<http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NORC>)) and the vice president is [Alejandro Moreno](<http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alejandro_Moreno>) ([Reforma](<http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reforma>) , in [Mexico](<http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mexico>)).Among the former presidents of WAPOR are [Juan Linz](<http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Juan_Linz>), [Elisabeth Noelle-Neumann](<http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elisabeth_Noelle-Neumann>), [Robert Worcester](<http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Worcester>) and [Seymour Martin Lipset](<http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seymour_Martin_Lipset>). The 64th WAPOR 2011 conference will be held in Amsterdam, The Netherlands, September 21-23. The conference theme of 2011 emphasizes how the Internet provides challenges and opportunities for public opinion scholars and how the internet is changing public opinion.
