# WML releases massive COVID-19 dataset

Dr. LIU Xiaofan, teamed with Prof. XU Xiao-Ke (Dalian Minzu University) and Prof. WU Ye (Beijing Normal University), released [a massive dataset](<https://www.nature.com/articles/s41597-021-00844-8>) containing detailed epidemiological information of more than 14,000 COVID-19 cases on [Scientific Data](<https://www.nature.com/articles/s41597-021-00844-8>) today.

The data curation team, composed of more than 20 research assistants from Beijing Normal University and Dalian Minzu University, has been monitoring the online daily COVID-19 reports from Chinese health committees (except Hubei province) since early 2020. The dataset contains the movement trajectories, suspected contact scenarios, and epidemiological history of more than 14,000 cases. Over the past year, the dataset has proven to be a valuable asset for pandemic research and supported publications in many top journals, including Science, Clinical Infectious Disease, and Emerging Infectious Disease.

This open-access dataset is stored synchronously on GitHub and figshare data repositories and is continuously updated on a bi-weekly basis. Here, we would like to call on researchers and interested parties worldwide to visit our [GitHub](<https://abcdefg3381.github.io/COVID_19_China_case_reports/>) website, join the data processing team, and help us check, correct, update, and maintain the dataset. We believe that this dataset would for sure lay a solid ground for future scientific research.

The Chinese version of this dataset has been previously released by The Paper press (澎湃). For details, please click this [link](<http://h5.thepaper.cn/html/zt/2020/12/liudiao/index.html>).

Moreover, the research team studied the mechanism, development, and control of the pandemic from multiple perspectives such as data mining, mathematical modeling, and computer simulation. The results have been published in journals such as Clinical Infectious Disease and Global Media Studies (see the list below for papers that are already published or released online).
