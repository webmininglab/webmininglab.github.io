# Winson and Jonathan Unleashed an Agenda-setting Theory 3.0

_Communication Research,_ one of the “Big Five” journals in the field, has recently published a study by lab members Winson and Jonathan ([Peng & Zhu, 2022](<https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/00936502221125067>)) on public agenda dynamics in the U.S. over the last 60 years.

![](https://webmininglab.github.io/assets/uploads/2022/10/CR_screenshot-1-1024x341.png)

The study represents a latest addition to the research literature on agenda-setting that dates back to 50 years ago when [McCombs and Shaw (1972)](<https://academic.oup.com/poq/article-pdf/36/2/176/5242655/36-2-176.pdf?casa_token=KuWSln9Hab4AAAAA:7lTyxrsFdg0DOopT730aMey_slKdpFRxLRDy0QwcFUzAMDqdUouBIxHYdMrmKPgcWEKZggXea41S>) published their seminal Chapel Hill study. On the 20th anniversary of the classic, Jonathan proposed a zero-sum theory ([Zhu, 1992](<https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/abs/10.1177/107769909206900403>)), introducing issue competition as a primary causal mechanism for agenda-setting. Another 20+ years later, Winson and Jonathan teamed up with a group of computer scientists to replicate issue competition on social media ([Xu et al., 2013](<https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/iel7/2945/6634084/06634134.pdf?casa_token=Ub7UEVh_eQ0AAAAA:EwWpE_UUjjCSCCHRHFdmINUoBGnx_RjIi_Hw4LF9pwISY-TTZ--dfNpC064gABFW48XMJn8>)) and add issue cooperation to form a coopetition mechanism on social media ([Sun et al., 2014](<https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/iel7/2945/6935054/06875992.pdf?casa_token=bNQkrencf_UAAAAA:o2pz9h1jK7ndY_DHiBQewhhq-7BVcliDv8LGE9_tZ8A3Tityb2DHpoi9UXW5DHBWNZlKGcA>)). In their latest chapter of the never-ending story on agenda-setting, Winson and Jonathan considered three mechanisms (i.e., competition, cooperation, and coexistence) but found competition to prevail over cooperation and coexistence ([Peng & Zhu, 2022](<https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/00936502221125067>)).

“We will hold an online symposium in early December this year to celebrate the 50th anniversary of agenda-setting theory”, Jonathan disclosed. “There will be three generations of agenda-setting researchers to recount their personal engagements with this ever-growing theory. Stay tuned for the official announcement.”
